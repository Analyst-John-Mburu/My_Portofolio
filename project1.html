This project involves the creation of a comprehensive Power BI dashboard based on a Spotify dataset, aimed at uncovering meaningful insights into music consumption behavior, user engagement, and genre trends. It was developed during your internship with Bharat Intern and showcases both technical expertise and analytical storytelling.

Objective

  • To explore listening patterns: When and how users stream music.
  • To analyze track popularity: Identify which songs and artists perform best.
  • To study genre preferences: Highlight shifts or dominance in musical genres.
  • To deliver actionable insights: For marketing, playlist curation, or artist promotion.

FEATURES OF THE DASHBOARD

  • Interactive Visualizations:
    • Bar and line charts showing top-streamed tracks and artists.
    • Genre distribution pie charts and heatmaps.
    • Time series plots of stream frequency.
  • User Engagement Metrics:
    • Stream counts over time.
    • Engagement by region or device (if available in dataset).
    • Popular release periods.
  • Custom Filters:
    • By genre, artist, time period, or track popularity.
    • Drill-through features to explore individual artist or album performance.

KEY INSIGHTS

  • Top Performing Artists and Tracks:
    • A small group of artists accounted for the majority of total streams, highlighting a power-law distribution in listener behavior.
    • Specific tracks consistently topped the charts across multiple time periods, indicating strong listener loyalty and viral impact.
  • Genre Popularity Trends:
    • Pop and Hip-Hop dominated streaming charts, especially among younger demographics.
    • Emerging genres such as Lo-fi and Indie showed steady growth, signaling a shift in audience tastes toward niche music.
  • Peak Streaming Periods:
    • Streaming activity peaked during weekends and evening hours, suggesting users engage with music primarily during leisure time.
    • Holiday seasons also experienced spikes, often aligning with new album or playlist releases.
  • User Engagement Patterns:
    • Tracks with higher engagement often featured collaborations or were part of curated playlists.
    • Shorter tracks tended to perform better, aligning with the modern trend toward quick content consumption.
  • Global Listening Behavior:
    • Regional preferences varied widely â€” Latin and K-pop genres gained significant traction in global markets.
    • Some tracks were globally popular, while others remained region-specific hits.
  • Release Strategy Insights:
    • Tracks released on Fridays tended to perform better, aligning with Spotifyâ€™s New Music Friday strategy.
    • Albums with a staggered release of singles built more anticipation and engagement.
